# Shooting script — Video 1: "I built an AI agent that predicts the World Cup winner"

Target length: 45–75s. Reuses steps 03 (spec), 04 (build), 05 (tools/data).

## Cold open (0–5s)
"I built an AI agent that predicts who wins the World Cup — and it plays out the entire bracket."

## Beat 1 — what it looks at (5–25s)
- Open the dashboard, **The Oracle** tab. The feature reel animates in.
- Line: "For every knockout tie it weighs the same things a real analyst would — team rating,
  recent form, goals, head-to-head, who's scoring, shots and possession, who's injured, rest
  days, the venue."
- Nuance: "No draws in a knockout, so it predicts who actually survives — extra time and pens
  included."

## Beat 2 — the simulation (25–60s)
- Hit **Play simulation**. Let the Round of 32 resolve, then 16, quarters, semis.
- Talk over it: "It takes the real bracket as it stands today — Brazil are already through —
  and plays every remaining game forward, advancing the winner each round."
- Call the drama: a marquee quarter-final, an upset the agent is backing, both sides of the
  draw closing toward the middle.

## Beat 3 — the champion (60–75s)
- The final resolves, the trophy lights up. Read the champion + the road they took.
- CTA: "Agree with the robot? I'll re-run it after every matchday."

## On camera / CLI alt
- `oracle simulate --mock --explain` for a terminal version of the same run.

## B-roll checklist
- [ ] Feature reel animating in
- [ ] Play button → bracket resolving round by round
- [ ] Champion reveal + road-to-the-final chips
- [ ] (optional) `oracle simulate` in the terminal
